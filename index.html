Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in Agricultural Economics at Texas A&M University (expected August 2026). My dissertation, Three Essays on Environmental and Development Economics, is advised by Dr. Kashi Kafle.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental, resource, and development economics. I study how environmental shocks, natural resource policies, and land restoration shape health, human capital, agricultural productivity, and household welfare across both developing and developed country contexts.

I apply causal inference, machine learning, and geospatial analysis to large-scale administrative and satellite data. My work draws on approaches such as difference-in-differences, event studies, and instrumental variables to generate policy-relevant evidence.

My research spans three interconnected areas: environmental policy and resource governance; climate risk, agriculture, and household adaptation; and environmental shocks, health, and human capital. Current projects examine rainfall shocks and education in Tanzania, land restoration in Ethiopia, environmental responses to mining bans, and the mental health effects of air pollution.

While much of my empirical work focuses on Sub-Saharan Africa, my research agenda is broadly motivated by questions that extend across both developing and developed economies.
